Professional Self-Assessment

Computer Science Growth and Readiness

Throughout the Computer Science program, I developed a stronger understanding of how software systems are designed, implemented, tested, and improved over time. My coursework gave me opportunities to work with programming logic, system architecture, data structures, database concepts, security considerations, and communication practices that are essential in professional software development environments.

The work in this portfolio demonstrates my ability to take an existing project, evaluate its strengths and limitations, and enhance it in ways that show measurable technical growth. By revisiting the Thermostat Project, I was able to move beyond simply meeting the original course requirements and focus on professional-quality improvements such as clearer structure, stronger documentation, more intentional data handling, and better alignment between design decisions and user needs.

My selected area of emphasis is software engineering, with attention to practical application design and maintainable implementation. The enhanced artifact reflects this focus by showing how design decisions, algorithmic thinking, and database-oriented planning can work together to create software that is easier to understand, extend, and evaluate.

This ePortfolio also demonstrates the communication skills required of a computer science professional. The code review, enhancement narratives, and self-assessment explain what changed, why the changes matter, and how the work aligns with the outcomes of the capstone. These materials show not only the completed technical work, but also the reasoning behind the enhancement process.
